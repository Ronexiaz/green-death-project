Alternative Death Care Options

In the advent of trying to foster a more death-positive or death-conscious society, knowing your options will always be the most important step. Listed below are five alternatives to conventional burial besides the more mainstream green or natural options.

Eternal Reefs:

An eternal reef is a biodegradable cement-adjacent object that helps foster life in the ocean. This is accomplished by taking the cremated remains of an individual and mixing them with the aforementioned product. The reef is then placed into the ocean by friends and/or family members where the reef will become a habitat for fish and other marine life.

Space Burial:

While it may sound absurd, space burial is a very real thing. A sample or all of a person’s cremated remains are shot into space where they will enjoy a trip up and back. These remains are not scattered throughout space, however, as to not contribute to the growing issue of space debris.

Aquamation:

While cremation is the use of fire and heat to turn human remains into ashes, aquamation is the use of water and movement to turn remains into a sort of ultra-fertile slurry. This process usually involves loading remains into a large tumbler with water and sloshing the mixture around until the desired result is reached.

Body Composting:

Body composting is a more radical and technically efficient method of decomposing large amounts of human remains. Body composting involves the use of a large structure to put the remains into along with carbon-heavy biodegradable materials in order to speed up the process of decomposition. The resulting compost is a dense, nutrient, fertilizer that can be used to cultivate memorial trees or gardens.

Capsula Mundi:

Capsula Mundi is the proprietary practice of turning the deceased human body into a tree. This is accomplished through the use of biodegradable seeded pods that utilize the nutrients provided by the remains to sprout and eventually grow into whatever tree requested within the particular climate.

If any of these alternatives remotely pique your interest, you are heavily encouraged to do your own research and find out what the proper death care solution is for you and your loved-ones.
